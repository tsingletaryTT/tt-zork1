## Core Experimental Principles

**Rule 1: TRY THE UNEXPECTED**
- Normal: "open mailbox"
- Experimental: "kick mailbox" → Does it respond?

**Rule 2: TEST PARSER BOUNDARIES**
- Complex commands: "put the brass lamp in the jeweled egg in the trophy case"
- Unusual verbs: "lick window", "smell grue", "sing to troll"
- Edge cases: "take all then drop all then take all"

**Rule 3: CREATIVE PROBLEM-SOLVING**
- Standard: "open door with key"
- Experimental: "break door with sword" OR "climb through window instead"

**Rule 4: ITEM ABUSE**
- "wear lamp as hat" (Is it wearable?)
- "eat sword" (Test object properties)
- "throw everything into case" (Bulk deposit?)
- "light rug on fire" (Destructive testing)

**Rule 5: SEQUENCE BREAKS**
- "go underground without lamp" (Planned death for testing)
- "ignore thief entirely" (Skip encounter?)
- "never use trophy case" (Alternative win condition?)
- "collect treasures in reverse order" (Non-optimal path)

## Decision-Making Model

**Every command should be surprising**:
- ✓ "tie rope to house" → Testing physics
- ✓ "read sword" → Unusual verb-noun combo
- ✓ "open egg" → Can treasures be opened?
- ✓ "dig with bare hands" → Testing implicit verbs
- ✗ "take lamp" → Too normal, too boring
- ✗ "go north" → Predictable exploration

**Experimental Hierarchy** (what to try first):
1. **Absurd but possible**: "put house in mailbox"
2. **Creative alternatives**: "break window" instead of "open window"
3. **Edge cases**: "take lamp. drop lamp. take lamp. drop lamp." (loop test)
4. **Unusual verbs**: "taste", "juggle", "dance", "whisper"
5. **Meta-commands**: "debug", "score -1", "inventory overflow"
